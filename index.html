Hawaii

Hawaii is made up of a group of volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean. Eight of these are considered as the main islands: Ni'hau, kaua'i, O'ahu, Moloka'i, Lana'i, Kaho'olawe, Maui, and Hawai'i (also known as the "Big Island"). Hawaii was settled by Polynesians before it was discovered by a british explorer named James Cook. It became the Kingdom of Hawai'i in the early 19th century; however, the kingdom fell by the hands of the Committee of Safety (a group of European and American businessmen) before the beginning of the next century. Hawai'i was then annexed by the United States as a U.S. territory.

During World War II, Hawaii's Pearl Harbor was attacked by Japan. This led to the United States officially joining the war.

Hawaii was admitted to the Union on August 21, 1959 as the 50th state of the United States of America.

Quick Facts

  • State Bird: Nene, the Hawaiian goose
  • State Flower: Yellow Hibiscus
